Welcome to Good Bull API!

Here you can browse all kinds of course data at your leisure, using a couple of simple API routes!

For the sake of consistency across the API and for the intended userbase, I've used terminology that Texas A&M students use. For those of you who aren't as familiar with this kind of terminology, hopefully this will help clarify meaning.

  • Course - This is a group of Sections on a particular subject that last a whole semester.
  • Section - This is what students register for and what they turn their work in to. It's a group of Meetings.
  • Meeting - This is a specific time, day, and location that a student physically shows up to.

FAQs

Where and how are you getting your data?

What a great question! I scraped it. Every last bit of it. Every grade distribution, section, course, and instructor you see, I scraped and aggregated from sources scattered across the Texas A&M University network. All of these sources come from a tamu.edu domain, so I'd say it's straight from the horse's mouth. For Courses, I use the course catalog. For Sections, I use Compass, which is a website written by Enterprise Information Systems, a department at Texas A&M. For grade distribution data, I parse the registrar's report. For buildings, I use Texas A&M's Mapping and Space Information.

How up-to-date is your data?

Different pieces of data are automatically updated at different frequencies, depending on how often I picture the data changing. Here are the arbitrary frequencies I've picked, and a little explanation as to why I picked them:

  • Courses - Courses are scraped once a week. The public-facing Course catalog is quite fast at responding, which means scraping is usually completed after five minutes or so.
  • Sections - Depends on whether it's a shallow or a deep scrape.
    • Shallow scrape - Shallow scrapes only update the eight most-recent terms, and are performed every thirty minutes.
    • Deep scrape - The last time I bothered to check, there were 268,389 different sections scraped from Spring 2009 to Fall 2018. Almost all of that information is not going to change, which is why deep scrapes only happen once.
  • Grade distributions - Grade distributions are scraped once every two months. This is more frequently than I'd like to scrape them, as the release schedule indicated on the registrar's website says that the grade distributions are updated once per semester, but no date is given, so I can't ever be sure that new information is available.
  • Buildings - Buildings are scraped once per semester. Buildings aren't really something that are constantly changing, so rescraping buildings occurs infrequently.

Why did you build this?

I'm definitely not the first person to write a course scraper, but the one thing that all of these scrapers don't have is a dependable public API. The fact that there is so much data at Texas A&M University, and the fact that none of it is able to be easily consumed seems to me like an extreme oversight. I plan on using this API in my own applications for statistical analysis and schedule planning, but you're more than welcome to use it. There's no authentication, no rate-limiting, just free and open data. As it should be. Cheers!

Your routes aren't meeting my needs, can you make...

I have only built the routes that I considered crucial to scheduling (which was the original intent of this project). If you think I've overlooked a critical piece of functionality that specifically has to do with data (i.e. things not sourced directly from the university), you are more than welcome to build it yourself, and submit a pull request! I'm very open (and actually encourage) the collection of more data, and I'd love some help with this project.
